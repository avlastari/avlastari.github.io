Why Communities are Awesome

And how they can inspire us to reveal our potential

Teamwork Inspiration Love for Learning

Discovering my passion

My passion for digital product design didn’t start in a classroom or a job. It started back in 2017, by chance, while volunteering with a community called Ethelon. I was helping at an event called Digitized — a conference about innovation and product design.

I still remember the moment clearly: my brain popped. I was fascinated. I spent the whole evening running from one lecture to the next, absorbing everything I could. It totally made sense: a design process built on real needs and data, creating solutions that made people’s lives simpler. That night, I fell in love with design and this community was the spark.

Communities teach as how to be accountable

Since then, I’ve joined the online academy IxDF, where I learned and — most importantly — got inspired about the importance of design thinking and problem-solving. Communities have always been my favorite space for learning and sharing.

But communities aren’t just about learning — they’re about practice. On Hype4 Academy community, I am pushing myself through challenges like the 90 Days of UI Design. That daily discipline teaches me consistency, creativity, and how to build strong design habits.

Finally, as it is always nice to be close to each other, I’m part of UX Athens Meetup, a team of designers from different companies in the city. We visit workspaces, share insights from different business fields, and learn from each other’s processes. These sessions are more than meetups — they’re small windows into how design culture grows in different environments.

Sharing locally, growing globally

Finally, I want to mention the 30u30 Program — a scholarship from Deutsche Telekom that not only helped me grow as a professional but also taught me the true value of learning from others.

This community brings together 17 professionals from diverse backgrounds and countries, each with their own perspective and story. Working and exchanging ideas with them has helped me learn the Business, see the bigger picture, grow faster, and stay inspired.

I’m deeply grateful for this group — they remind me every day that growth happens when we share, collaborate, and lift each other up..

Communities are awesome because they make you realize you’re not alone in this journey. They’ve fueled my curiosity, kept me disciplined, and connected me with mentors, peers, and friends who constantly challenge me to grow.For me, design is never a solo act. It’s always about people — whether they’re customers, teammates, or a community of designers building the future together.
