From Project Management to Scrum Agility

Why the combination of those 2 form a superpower

Project Management Design Thinking Agile Mindset

Two mindsets, one designer

Traditional project management and Scrum are often seen as opposites. But I see them as complementary mindsets. PM taught me discipline and structure; Scrum taught me adaptability and iteration. Together, they shape how I approach every project:

• Always mindful of the big picture.
• Always ready to adapt and improve quickly.

From retail to digital design

I began my career in retail design and construction, where project management was crucial. Deadlines were strict, budgets tight, and nothing could slip. Every day mattered. If a store reopening was delayed, even by one day, it meant lost revenue.

This experience gave me a mindset of planning carefully, communicating constantly, and respecting constraints. I worked closely with construction teams, learning how to align schedules, anticipate risks, and keep projects moving without disruption.

Both worlds — project management and agile — have shaped me. The discipline of PM ensures I never lose sight of the big picture. The flexibility of Scrum ensures that I can move quickly and get things done by learning and improving every step of the way. For me, it’s not about choosing one or the other, but about applying the right mindset to the right challenge.

Design Thinking and agile as a mindset

As a UX/UI designer, I’ve worked for years in agile squads and I don’t wait for the perfect scope to start. I collaborate in sprints, apply design thinking, and build MVPs that are tested quickly with users. This accelerates learning and ensures decisions are shaped by data, not assumptions.

Agile values — transparency, feedback, adaptation — create the best environment for design. They let me:

  • Validate ideas directly with customers.
  • Iterate designs rapidly.
  • Collaborate seamlessly with developers and product owners.
  • Deliver added value at every sprint—not just at the end of a project.
