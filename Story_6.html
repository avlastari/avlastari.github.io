Telling Stories through Wireframes

that are telling a story

UX Design Wireframes Concept Design

Everybody wants to see wireframes in a designer’s portfolio.

But here’s the truth: in real life, nobody gets excited about a sketchy, grayscale draft — and I totally get it. It’s often hard for non‑designers to connect with those screens or imagine the final intent behind them. That’s why I always try to add context, visuals, and storytelling even at the wireframing stage.

Sure, creativity starts on paper — but why not make our wireframes more visually appealing and more human?

Case Study: inspiration from a voice message

Back in 2023, when I was working in the chatbot chapter, I had the chance to experiment with a concept design that went a step beyond everyday work. The idea was to create a new chatbot for our youth prepaid product — one of the most popular services among students — often acting as the “front door” to our core products.

We wanted something bold yet playful, a chatbot experience that matched the energy of its audience. That insight changed our direction: the chatbot could double as a search bar, indexing products and results while maintaining a conversational tone.

Testing the hypothesis

We quickly created a beta version and tested it with a small group of users. We observed that they weren’t “chatting” — they wanted quick, functional answers. That insight confirmed our direction.

Pushing the idea further

What if the chatbot could serve as a search tool, yet become a social conversational app? We explored: enabling gifting and playful interactions, adding customization and gamification, and giving friends a reason to return.

For me, this project proved that wireframes are not just boxes and lines — they can be a story people can actually feel.
