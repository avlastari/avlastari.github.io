Accessibility at heart

Design for Inclusivity and Impact

UX Design Accessibility Inclusive Design

Two mindsets, one designer

This year, my company launched a project to comply with the Digital Accessibility Act and WCAG standards. For me, it was more than a requirement — it was a project very close to my heart.

Early on, I realized that accessibility makes a difference not just technically, but ethically and socially. Inclusive design means making sure everyone can access digital products — and that’s the kind of impact I want my work to have.

My contributions

As part of this project, I:

  • Educated myself on WCAG principles and best practices.
  • Worked with my team to revise brand colors on Digital properties for proper contrast.
  • Contributed to alterations in the design system, ensuring accessibility is baked in at the component level, not just in one-off fixes.
  • Helped map and track accessibility violations across our digital ecosystem.
  • Conducted audits and user tests on our website.
  • Even learned basic accessibility-related coding to understand how fixes were implemented.
Accessibility has become one of the most meaningful aspects of my design practice. It reminds me that design is never neutral — it either includes or excludes. My goal is always to design products that invite everyone in.

Impact across Products

Over the past year, I’ve contributed to audits and accessible design updates for four different digital products. Each one taught me something new about balancing usability, aesthetics, and accessibility. And it’s not just about client work: this portfolio itself is built with accessibility in mind — simple, structured, and ally-friendly by design.
